## Hypothesis to test (vs v5)

Tier-3 (insertion) is bimodal across v1ŌĆōv4. Two interventions stacked in v8:
1. LoRA rank 8ŌåÆ32 to break the capacity ceiling.
2. Velocity control mode instead of pose ŌĆö error-driven Cartesian command, doesn't suffer from controller-lag pose-integration.

**Why absolute encoding (paired with velocity)**: reading `aic_utils/aic_xvla/aic_xvla/handler.py:65-80` + `X-VLA/datasets/utils.py:99-104` + `aic_utils/aic_xvla/aic_xvla/eval.py:147-148`, delta training learns command-relative deltas (proprio = action target at chunk start) but inference adds **live state**. With controller lag, inferred target = lagged_state + small_delta Ōēł lagged_state. Combining delta with velocity would compound (small delta ŌåÆ small twist ŌåÆ freeze). Absolute pairs cleanly: model emits absolute target, twist = (abs ŌłÆ live)/dt = correct error signal. v5 (delta+velmode) is the experiment for the alternative arm; v8 is the cleaner-reasoning bet.
